Representative User Stories
As a CEO, I want to upload monthly revenue data and immediately know the biggest risks and opportunities.
As a Finance Manager, I want the system to automatically calculate KPIs so I don't build spreadsheets manually.
As a Business Analyst, I want to ask follow-up questions in natural language instead of writing SQL.
As a Sales Manager, I want recommendations on which regions or products need attention.

UX Principles
Minimize clicks between upload and first insight.
Explain every recommendation with supporting evidence.
Use business language instead of statistical jargon.
Keep advanced analytics available without overwhelming beginners.
Maintain transparency by exposing underlying metrics.
Information Architecture
Primary navigation:• Home Dashboard• Dataset Workspace• Insights• Chat Assistant• Reports• SettingsEach uploaded dataset becomes an independent workspace containing charts, KPIs, conversations and reports.
Experience Goals
First insight generated within minutes.
Conversational interface feels like speaking to an experienced analyst.
Visualizations complement explanations instead of replacing them.
Every important insight includes confidence and supporting metrics.
Accessibility & Usability
The interface should prioritize readability, keyboard accessibility, responsive layouts, consistent terminology and clear error messages. Color should never be the only indicator of business status or alerts.
Future Personas
Future enterprise versions may support data engineers, executives, compliance officers, customer success teams, marketing analysts and HR managers through domain-specific analytical modules.

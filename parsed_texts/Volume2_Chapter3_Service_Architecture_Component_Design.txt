6. Cross-Cutting Concerns
Structured logging with request IDs.
Authentication before workspace access.
Authorization on every dataset operation.
Metrics collection for each pipeline stage.
Caching of analytical context for repeated chat queries.
Versioned prompts and model configurations.
7. Scalability Strategy
Each logical service should be independently scalable. CPU-intensive analytics and report generation should execute through background workers. AI requests should be isolated from deterministic analytics to prevent bottlenecks.
8. Future Decomposition
As adoption grows, the Analytics Engine can be separated into KPI, Forecasting, Anomaly Detection and Time-Series services. The AI Service can evolve into a multi-model orchestration layer supporting specialized reasoning models.
9. Engineering Decisions
Prefer service boundaries over monolithic business logic.
Keep business rules outside prompt templates whenever possible.
Persist reusable analytical artifacts.
Treat AI as a consumer of analytics, not a replacement.

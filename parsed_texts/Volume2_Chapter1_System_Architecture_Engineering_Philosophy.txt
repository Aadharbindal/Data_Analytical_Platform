1. Purpose
This document establishes the engineering foundation of the AI Business Analyst Platform. It translates the business requirements from Volume 1 into a scalable technical architecture that can evolve from an MVP into an enterprise-grade AI analytics platform.
2. Engineering Goals
Build a modular service-oriented architecture.
Keep deterministic analytics separate from LLM reasoning.
Support future domain-specific analytical modules.
Enable maintainability, observability and scalability.
Design APIs that are AI-agent friendly.
3. Design Philosophy
The platform follows a computation-first architecture. Statistical calculations, profiling, KPI generation and validation are completed before invoking any LLM. Language models explain results rather than invent them. Every recommendation should be traceable to computed evidence.

6. Architecture Principles
Single Responsibility per service.
Stateless APIs where possible.
Structured context passed to LLMs.
Replaceable AI model providers.
Evidence-first recommendations.
Versioned prompts and APIs.

8. Architecture Decision Records (Initial)
ADR-001: Use FastAPI for backend APIs.
ADR-002: Keep analytics deterministic before LLM invocation.
ADR-003: Store dataset metadata separately from raw files.
ADR-004: Design services to allow future microservice extraction.
9. Non-Goals
Do not build a generic chatbot.
Do not tightly couple analytics with LLM prompts.
Do not require manual dashboard creation.
Do not optimize for every business domain in Version 1.

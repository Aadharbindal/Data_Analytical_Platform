1. Objective
This chapter documents the key architectural decisions behind the platform. Every major technology and design choice is recorded with its rationale, alternatives considered, consequences and future review criteria.
2. ADR Process
Each Architecture Decision Record (ADR) follows a consistent structure: Decision ID, Status, Context, Decision, Alternatives Considered, Consequences, Risks, and Review Trigger.

3. Engineering Principles
Prefer simplicity over premature optimization.
Keep business logic independent of LLM prompts.
Use strongly typed APIs and schemas.
Design for observability from day one.
Maintain backward compatibility for public APIs.
Automate testing wherever practical.

5. Governance Model
Architecture changes should be proposed through new ADRs, reviewed by technical leads, validated through prototypes where appropriate, and approved before implementation. Deprecated decisions should remain documented to preserve historical context.
6. Review Triggers
Major performance bottlenecks.
New enterprise requirements.
Cloud migration.
AI model replacement.
Regulatory or security changes.
7. Technical Debt Policy
Technical debt should be explicitly tracked, prioritized and linked to roadmap items. Temporary shortcuts must include documented remediation plans and target milestones.
8. Coding Standards
PEP 8 for Python.
Type hints for public interfaces.
OpenAPI-first endpoint documentation.
Consistent logging format.
Unit and integration tests for critical modules.
9. Long-Term Architecture Evolution
As adoption grows, the modular monolith can gradually evolve into independently deployable services without changing public APIs. AI components may later support multiple model providers and specialized analytical agents.

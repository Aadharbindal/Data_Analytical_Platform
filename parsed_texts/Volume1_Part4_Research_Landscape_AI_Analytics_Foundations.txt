Purpose
This chapter summarizes the major research directions that influence the architecture of the AI Business Analyst Platform. Rather than implementing a chatbot over CSV files, the platform should combine deterministic analytics with language-model reasoning, an approach increasingly explored in academic and industrial research.

Key Research Insights
Deterministic statistical calculations should precede LLM reasoning.
Automatic Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) provides structured context before insight generation.
Grounding responses in computed metrics reduces hallucinations.
Evaluation should consider correctness, usefulness and actionability—not only natural language quality.
Business recommendations should reference measurable evidence from the dataset.

Architectural Principles Derived from Research
Separate computation from explanation.
Maintain traceability between every insight and supporting metrics.
Design modular analytical services.
Keep prompts structured and reproducible.
Support future replacement of individual AI models without redesigning the platform.
Research-Informed Pipeline
Recommended analytical workflow:Dataset Upload→ Validation→ Cleaning→ Profiling→ Exploratory Data Analysis→ KPI Computation→ Statistical Findings→ Insight Generation→ Recommendation Engine→ Executive Summary→ Conversational Follow-up
Open Research Questions
How should insight quality be measured objectively?
How can recommendations remain explainable and auditable?
When should statistical models be preferred over LLM reasoning?
How should conflicting analytical signals be presented to business users?
Implications for Version 1
The first version should prioritize correctness, transparency and reproducibility over advanced autonomy. Every generated insight should be traceable to measurable statistics computed from the uploaded dataset.
